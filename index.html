STAR

Speech-to-Audio Generation with Sound Event-Related Representations

|Paper|Code|

Zeyu Xie, Xuenan Xu, Mengyue Wu

X-LANCE Lab, Shanghai Jiao Tong University

Abstract.

In recent years, research on audio generation has experienced rapid development, with significant attention given to text-, audio- and vision-based audio generation. However, speech, as a natural mode of interaction, has not received adequate attention in audio generation studies. This work aims to fill this gap to endow models with stronger interactive capabilities by proposing a speech-to-audio generation system. Specifically, our initial step is to demonstrate through representation learning experiments that sound event-related representations can be extracted from speech, which can be used to guide audio generation. Utilizing the semantic information contained in these representations, we propose first end-to-end generation model from speech to audio. Additionally, we introduce a bridge network for representation mapping and devise a three-stage training strategy to optimize performance. Experimental result shows that our proposed model significantly outperforms the baseline model that does not utilize semantic representations, and it is also comparable to several cascaded systems. An overview of STAR framework and training strategy


For an input speech utterance, sound event-related representations are extracted by the speech encoder and then mapped by the bridge network to semantic features S. The latent diffusion model generates the latent, which is decoded back to the waveform, conditioning on S. The training comprises three stages. The bridge network is pre-trained in the first stage and fine-tuned in the second stage. Finally the diffusion model is fine-tuned in the third stage.
